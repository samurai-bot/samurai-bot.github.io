§ Doctrine

I work where infrastructure meets governance.

I started in 2005 wiring up wholesale billing for Tier-1 carriers — the kind of system where a misplaced tariff costs someone a quarter of revenue. That apprenticeship in consequence shaped everything since: real-time charging at Openet, online charging at Huawei and Celcom, the BSS/OSS rebuilds at Maxis and SPTel, and now the data & AI platform at M1.

What I do well: take a complex, regulated stack — engineers, vendors, auditors, executives, and the inevitable legacy — and turn it into something that ships, scales, and doesn’t embarrass anyone on a Monday morning. I write architecture, but I also write code, run platforms, and sit through the steering committee at the end of the quarter.

  • 01

    Governance by design.

    Lineage, RBAC, and quality checks belong in the pipeline, not a slide deck.

  • 02

    Boring, then brilliant.

    Get the boring fundamentals — schemas, SLAs, FinOps — right before you bolt on the agents.

  • 03

§ Selected work

A short tour of the last decade.

  1. 2025— M1 Limited

    An enterprise data & AI platform, governance-first.

    Built and led the team standing up M1’s data lakehouse on Databricks & Snowflake, with Kafka-driven CDC pipelines feeding near real-time decisioning. Layered on an AI Center of Excellence shipping reconciliation agents and GenAI-to-SQL tools that put data in the hands of non-technical teams.

    • 30% platform OPEX reduction via partition pruning & compute right-sizing.
    • Zero-trust data environment with column-level lineage and automated DR.
    • PDPA-aligned governance frameworks accepted by audit and regulators.
  2. 2023—25 M1 Limited

    Re-platforming the systems that bill millions of customers daily.

    As Head of Enterprise Architecture, ran the migration of monolithic Billing and Order Management to cloud-native microservices on AKS. ACID compliance, zero data loss, and a stack the digital channels could finally move quickly on top of.

    • Partnered with C-suite and top-tier vendors (Salesforce, Adobe, multiple SIs).
    • Hardened resilience & cybersecurity posture across the digital estate.
  3. 2018—21 SPTel

    A greenfield BSS/OSS for Singapore’s enterprise fibre player.

    Joined SPTel pre-launch and architected the end-to-end CRM, CPQ and Order Management stack for enterprise customers. Built the in-house engineering function from zero to fifteen — engineering standards, architectural governance, and the discipline to keep a young company running with carrier-grade availability.

  4. 2015—18 Maxis

    From overnight batch to sub-second balance updates.

    Application architect on the Maxis BSS transformation. Replaced the legacy CRM & Billing stack and engineered the cutover from batch to real-time, event-based charging — sub-second balance updates and a centralized product catalog that compressed time-to-market for new commercial launches.

  5. 2005—15 Apprenticeship years

    Celcom Axiata, Huawei, Openet, Intec / CSG.

    A decade of OCS, PCRF and wholesale billing — onsite at Tier-1 operators in Indonesia, France, the Netherlands and Malaysia, learning the discipline of high-volume systems where reconciliation is a business, not a feature.
